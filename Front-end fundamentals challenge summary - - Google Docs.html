Front-end fundamentals challenge summary - 
 
I had a tough time with CSS initially. Not in this project, just in general. From the first time it 
showed up in the curriculum. Then came JavaScript. 75% of JS made sense on some level and 
the rest just wouldn’t click for me for quite some time. I’m disappointed to say that I really 
allowed myself to be affected by the adversity it all possed. I got down on myself and started 
thinking that I just wasn’t wired to code. I would never be an effective UX/UI Designer. No way 
I’d ever get hired anywhere. I tell you this to let you know that everything seemed like a 
challenge when I finally started this challenge. Literally, every aspect of it seemed nigh 
impossible for me. 
 
Now, I understand it’s not perfect, not beautifully designed or terribly creative but I am really 
proud of this screen and also proud of myself. It’s like everything just kinda clicked and I knew 
what to do. The one thing I ran into an issue with that took me awhile was getting the top 
navigation bar aligned. I also assumed I’d be able to make every line number, destination and 
ETA a grid-item-1, 2 & 3 respectively. That wasn’t the case. Because the individual cells are 
relatively small and a good amount of info had to go in each one (and remain legible) there 
wasn’t much room for variations in the amount or size of each block of info if I wanted to just 
reuse the same 3 grid-item classes. I had to finagle a few extra .grid-items to accommodate the 
longer destination names and a few of the ETAs toward the end that say “Resumes 6:00 am” 
etc.  
 
There is one thing I couldn't nail down, and that is the apparent padding/ margins running 
down the sides of the whole screen. Initially I thought it might've been an issue with the status 
bar and map .png files that occurred somewhere in the migration from Figma. Then I realized 
that once aligned in the center they were both the exact same width (as one another and as 
their Figma counterparts) and the table cells were pixel for pixel the same size as their Figma 
forefathers. Everythings the same size as the sister elements in Figma and the lanes of margin 
down the left and right sides of the screen seemed to me (the novice who probably truly has no 
idea) to be a consequence of viewing the project in the Dev Tools Inspector. Perhaps it gives 
you just a little extra room in that view instead of sitting flush with the edges. I’m not sure, 
maybe someone can help me out with that. 
 
In conclusion, every piece of this projects development leading up to this first submission has 
been enjoyable, free of pressure and shockingly & pleasantly lacking in the ability to turn me into 
a frustrated, blood-eyed, code-cursing psycho. My favorite part about this little victory is that I 
didn’t have to reach out to my mentor, Slack or any tech coaches for help. Not once! I’m not 
above or opposed to asking for help in the slightest. It’s just great to feel some sense of 
self-sufficiency in navigating what until very recently might as well have been an alien language. 
I’m proud of myself. Thats a priceless feeling. More importantly I’m very, very grateful for this 
curriculum, the tech coaches and my mentor. Thanks Bloc, you’re changing my life.
